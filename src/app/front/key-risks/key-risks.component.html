Key Risks for JustStake Investors

Investing in real estate through JustStake (“we,” “us,” “our”) offers unique opportunities but also carries inherent risks. This document outlines the key risks associated with investing through our Platform. By proceeding with any investment, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and accepted these risks.

1. General Investment Risks

a. Risk of Loss

All investments carry the risk of partial or complete loss of the invested amount. Past performance is not a guarantee of future returns.

b. Market Volatility

Real estate values may fluctuate due to changes in market conditions, demand, interest rates, or macroeconomic factors, which could impact the performance of your investment.

c. Liquidity Risk

Real estate is inherently illiquid. Although we offer bi-annual exit windows and secondary market opportunities, the ability to sell your investment is not guaranteed and may be subject to delays or reduced valuation.

d. Economic and Political Risks

Changes in economic policies, political instability, inflation, currency fluctuations, or regulatory changes in the regions where the properties are located could negatively affect your investment.

2. Real Estate-Specific Risks

a. Property Value Decline

The value of real estate properties may decline due to market saturation, declining demand, or neighborhood deterioration.

b. Rental Income Risk

Rental income may fluctuate or cease due to tenant defaults, vacancies, changes in rental demand, or legal restrictions.

c. Development Risks

Investments in development projects are subject to risks such as construction delays, cost overruns, and unforeseen complications.

d. Geographic Concentration

If your portfolio is concentrated in a specific location, it may be more vulnerable to local market downturns, natural disasters, or regulatory changes.

3. Regulatory and Compliance Risks

a. Legal and Regulatory Changes

Changes in laws or regulations governing real estate investments, taxes, or property ownership in the jurisdictions we operate in may adversely affect your investment.

b. Foreign Ownership Restrictions

Some jurisdictions may impose restrictions on foreign ownership of real estate, which could impact your rights as an investor.

c. Compliance with Local Laws

Investments are subject to local laws and regulations, and non-compliance could result in penalties, fines, or loss of property rights.

4. Operational Risks

a. Management Risk

The performance of your investment may depend on the effectiveness of property management services. Poor management can negatively impact rental income and property value.

b. Platform Risks

The operation of our Platform is subject to technological risks, including system outages, cyberattacks, and data breaches, which could impact your access to information or funds.

c. Reliance on Third Parties

We rely on third-party service providers for property sourcing, valuation, management, and other services. Failures or delays by these parties may adversely affect your investment.

5. Financial Risks

a. Interest Rate Risk

Changes in interest rates may affect the cost of financing for properties, impacting profitability and overall returns.

b. Currency Risk

For investors investing in properties located in foreign markets, currency exchange rate fluctuations could affect returns when converted back to your local currency.

c. Fee Structures

Management fees, performance fees, and other charges may reduce the net returns of your investment.

6. Tax Risks

a. Tax Liability

Investors are responsible for understanding and complying with the tax laws in their country of residence as well as the country where the property is located. Tax treatment may change over time.

b. Withholding Taxes

Investments in certain jurisdictions may be subject to withholding taxes on rental income or capital gains, which could impact net returns.

7. Environmental and Natural Disaster Risks

a. Natural Disasters

Properties may be at risk of damage or destruction due to natural disasters such as earthquakes, floods, or hurricanes. Insurance may not fully cover all losses.

b. Environmental Compliance

Properties may require remediation or compliance with environmental regulations, resulting in additional costs and potential fines.

8. Fraud and Misrepresentation Risks

Although we conduct due diligence on all investment opportunities, there is a risk of fraud, misrepresentation, or inaccurate property valuations by sellers or third-party providers.

9. Risk of Changes to JustStake’s Business Model

Changes to our business model, operations, or services, including termination of the Platform, could impact the management and performance of your investments.

10. Investor Responsibility

a. Due Diligence

Investors are encouraged to conduct their own due diligence and seek independent financial, legal, or tax advice before making any investment.

b. Diversification

Investors should consider diversifying their investments to mitigate risks associated with concentration in a single property, market, or asset class.

11. Disclaimer

JustStake does not guarantee the performance of any investment. Investments are subject to risks, including loss of capital. By using our Platform, you acknowledge that you are investing at your own risk and that JustStake shall not be held liable for any losses.

12. Updates to Key Risks

We may update this Key Risks document periodically to reflect changes in our operations, services, or the regulatory environment. Please review this document regularly to stay informed.
